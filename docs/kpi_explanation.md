# KPI Explanation – Supply Chain Control Tower

## 1. Inventory Level

Represents the total available stock across warehouses.
This KPI helps monitor stock availability and ensures that inventory is sufficient to meet demand.

---

## 2. Demand Forecast

Represents the predicted demand based on historical data and trends.
It is used for planning procurement and avoiding stock shortages.

---

## 3. Units Sold (Sales)

Represents actual product sales.
Used to compare against forecasted demand to evaluate forecasting accuracy.

---

## 4. Demand vs Sales Gap

Difference between forecasted demand and actual sales.

**Business Insight:**

* Positive gap → overestimation (excess inventory risk)
* Negative gap → underestimation (stockout risk)

---

## 5. Inventory Gap (Reorder Logic)

Difference between inventory level and reorder point.

**Business Insight:**

* Inventory < Reorder Point → replenishment required
* Helps prevent stockouts and maintain service levels

---

## 6. Profit

Calculated as:
Profit = Revenue - Cost

Used to evaluate supplier and product performance.

---

## 7. Profit Margin

Profit expressed as a percentage of revenue.

**Business Insight:**

* High margin → efficient sourcing / pricing
* Low margin → cost or pricing issue

---

## 8. Stockout Indicator

Flag indicating whether a product is out of stock.

**Business Insight:**

* Directly impacts customer satisfaction
* Helps identify supply chain inefficiencies

---

## 9. Supplier Performance

Measured using profit contribution and supply consistency.

**Business Insight:**

* Helps identify top-performing suppliers
* Supports supplier optimization decisions

---

## 10. Warehouse Contribution

Shows inventory distribution across warehouses.

**Business Insight:**

* Identifies overstocked or underutilized locations
* Helps in logistics and distribution optimization
